Mirchi Bajji

Description

Mirchi Bajji is a popular, quick & easy to make, and addictive Indian snack recipe that is crispy and crunchy on the outside.

Mirchi means green chilies, and bajji means deep-fried fritters. These are some of the best and most popular Indian recipes served with coffee/tea or as a side with your meal.

Ingredients

  • 8 Long green chilies
  • ½ teaspoon Salt
  • 1 cup Besan / Gram Flour / Chickpea Flour
  • ¼ teaspoon Hing / Asafoetida
  • ½ teaspoon Cumin seeds
  • ½ teaspoon Red chile powder
  • Water (approx. ½ to ¾ cup)
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Cut ¼ inch at the end and slit the green chilies lengthwise, leaving the portion near the top/stem undisturbed. You can discard the seeds or keep them as it is. Do not discard the stem.
  2. In a pan, add chickpea flour, hing, cumin seeds, red chile powder, and salt. Add water little by little and whisk to form a medium-thick lump-free batter.
  3. Heat oil in a pan for frying on medium flame. Once the oil is hot, dip each chile in the batter and then drop them in hot oil, three to four chilies at a time, depending on the size of the pan.
  4. Cook until it becomes crisp and golden brown on all sides, flipping it a couple of times during the process. The bhajis will take approximately 4 to 5 minutes to develop golden brown color and a crispy texture.
  5. Transfer the bhajis to a paper towel to absorb excess oil. Repeat the process for the remaining chilies
  6. Serve hot with a cup of coffee or tea, and enjoy.
